Voting Instructions

You have 100 voice credits to express your preferences. Each "vote" for an applicant costs credits based on how many votes you give them:

The first vote costs 1 credit. The second vote costs 3 more (4 total). The third vote costs 5 more (9 total). And so on.

Why quadratic? This system lets you express how strongly you feel. But expressing very strong preferences is expensive ‚Äî giving 10 votes to one applicant uses all 100 credits. This naturally balances passionate supporters against broader consensus.

Key improvement: Prevents single-issue voters from dominating. A member who cares deeply about one organization can show that, but the cost ensures they can't give 10 votes to 10 different organizations.
